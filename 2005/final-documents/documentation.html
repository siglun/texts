Introduction

The reason I write this package of documents is that I'm quitting. It is meant to be a self-contained package of documents to be consulted by colleagues who run or use stuff I built while working within Lund University Libraries.

This is the material that can be distributed freely. There are parts of this documentation that are sensitive and kept on secure areas of the server.

Conventions

It is a substantial amount of texts, and they are split into several parts. This document is meant to serve as a global table of contents for lot.

Electronic publishing

By electronic publishing I understand services that are meant to disseminate digital content and metadata about that content.

  1. XERXES 3
  2. Scripta Academia Lundensia 4

The two services are are closely tied together, and are documented as one integrated system. These services are currently running on balder.lub.lu.se, as a part of the Keystone DLS Hotel I have installed there. The Xerxes and Scripta services required some modifications in order to be possible to run from a single set of software. The modifications are documented in a separate document.

Still, the two e-publishing services are different in many ways. They have different history, and inherits data from earlier services and the maintenance of older data is different. For Scripta there is still a need to maintain data from the old dissdb.

Masters theses are produced en mass, and for some departments it the sheer volume makes it worthwhile to have a local service. Thus harvested data constituted a substantial input to Xerxes. To that end there is a special Xerxes Bot.

A few departments take advantage of the list courses facility

Cataloging

A cataloging service is a service which is meant for cataloging, or for making a catalogue available in digital form. The three services listed below are very different, but still they share important characteristics.

  1. Jarring 6
  2. Ediffah 7
  3. Xerxes and Scripta8

As noted above, Xerxes and Scripta share important characteristics. As far as cataloging is concerned they share metadata system. Since they share metadata definition they also share cataloging tool.
