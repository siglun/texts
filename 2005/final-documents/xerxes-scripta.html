Design principles

The two seemingly different services are basically just one, the design build on the following principles

  • The two services build on a modular philosophy, i.e., that different components should be kept separate and thus be possible to replace independent of each other.
  • Furthermore, it is an advantage to store metadata in a portable format together with the objects they describe.
  • Stored objects may have an hierarchical structure, i.e., they may have components that should be possible to describe and archive independently of the host object.
  • It should at least be possible to simultaneously support different editorial workflows in a number of different services and still have the advantage of maintaining a range of end user interfaces with a minimum of differences.

Users & Agents

Roles for persons in Xerxes Cataloguing Toolkit
userauthor

An author who is also a user of the system. userauthors are athenticated vi the university's LDAP system

reviewer

A reviewer is a person with the power to publish or withdraw objects from the public service.

The access rights are limited to a certain jurisdiction, which for Xerxes is arranged hierarchically according to administrative domains as defined by the university's organisation. In Scripta there is a flat list of jurisdictions based on faculty.

A reviewer may also edit metadata, and has the right to download and upload objects from and to the object store.

admin

An admin has the same rights as a reviewer, but may edit peoples roles, and may also change his or her own jurisdiction.

otherauthor

Otherauthors are authors that has to be registered, but are not present in the university's directory service.

retired

A retired staff (former reviewers or admins)

exporter

A software agent used for exporting data from a certain jurisdiction or subject domain. Not currently used.
