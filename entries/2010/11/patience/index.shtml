On patience and bus stops

Sigfrid Lundberg's Stuff 2010-11-26

Waiting is not for everyone.

Patience, states The Mirror (Saturday 19, 1825), is the art of waiting. Time passes quickly with him who hopes for days and lives for the morrow. Hope has such efficacy that it can lead us to the end of life through an agreeable path and even beyond life itself.

Hope and optimism helps. So long as we still have the faith that a bus will eventually arrive and pick us up, so long will we also patiently stay at a bus stop. However, pragmatism helps as well. I'm convinced that when waiting you should eventually give upp the hope after a certain period.

I have a lot of patience, usually more that what is good for me. All of a sudden I've been standing at the bus stop longer than it takes to walk the same distance.
