On being an international commuter

Sigfrid Lundberg's Stuff 2009-07-21

In both Lund and Copenhagen there are a lot of people riding their bicycles. They behave very differently, however, and their spatial distribution is different. In Copenhagen the riders have a much more aggregated distribution. They ride at high speed in herds, somewhat like Mongolian cavalry. Before their attacks against the pedestrians, their primary targets, the Copenhagen bicycle riders congregate at traffic lights. In Lund, where the where the density of traffic lights is lower, we bicycle riders tend to have a more solitary life style.

Currently I usually leave home ten past seven. That gives me ample of time to catch the 07.39 train from Lund to Copenhagen. Except for occasional encounters with dog owners and an increasing number of power walking young ladies that, trip is uneventful. When it isn't raining. The power walkers look as if they hate walking; they seem to be in a terrible hurry to get home for a shower and a nice slow breakfast. I don't blame them. For some reason, the power walking ladies never appear when it is raining, as far as I can tell from the bus.

The only really serious problem is to find a secure parking place for my bike. A lot of fellow bicycle riders who park there have lost their vehicles there, through theft. I've recently aquired a new bike, since my previous one was stolen outside our home. The first temporal bottle-neck is to lock the thing. I've got two solid locks, and try to park where I can use one of them for locking the bike to an iron structure (see the image).
