On being an international commuter

Sigfrid Lundberg's Stuff 2009-07-21

In both Lund and Copenhagen there are a lot of people riding bicycles. They behave very differently, however, and their spatial distribution is different. In Copenhagen the riders have a much more aggregated distribution. They ride at high speed in squadrons, somewhat like Mongolian cavalry. Before their mounted attacks against the pedestrians on H.C. Andersens Boulevard, their prime target, the Copenhagen bicycle riders congregate at traffic lights.

In Lund, where the where the traffic lights are more scarce, there are few natural meeting points. In Lund bicycle riders operate in much smaller units, or solitarily. Great speed and bravery is nevertheless needed, as Lund's bicycle riders are well known for their suicidal attacks upon four-wheeled vehicles.

Currently I usually leave home and mount my bike ten past seven in the morning. That gives me ample time to catch the 07.39 train from Lund to Copenhagen. Except for occasional encounters with dog owners and an increasing number of power walking young ladies the trip is uneventful. The power walkers stride along in a frenzy, their eyes fixed at a point on the horizon. Neither their faces nor their eyes reveal any emotion whatsoever, other than utter dislike for walking. I honestly believe that they are all in a terrible hurry home for a shower and a nice slow breakfast. What else could it be? They are completely absent when I return home in the afternoon. Or when it's raining.

A parking place

I have one serious problem. That is to find a secure parking place for my bike at the railway station. A lot of people who park there have lost their vehicles. The rumour tells stories about thieves coming there with lorries, stealing bicycles for export to Poland. I've recently acquired a new bike, since my previous one was stolen. But that was outside our home. Apart from finding the parking place, the first temporal bottle-neck is to lock the thing. I've got two solid locks, and try to park where I can use one of them for locking the bike to the iron structure provided by the local council for the benefit for of paranoid bicycle owners (see the image). They are always taken before I get one.

That would describe the normal sequence of events, but right now they replacing 12 kilometers of tracks. Hence the railway which is normally double track is now running on half its normal capacity. I could dwell on the inconveniences caused by this, but I'll spare you that pain. The normal state of affairs is that I after about an hour on the train. Right now it's more often one and a half.

Two cultures
