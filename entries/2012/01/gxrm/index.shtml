100% M

Sigfrid Lundberg's Stuff 2012-01-17

Everything there is M mount, but there is no real Leica stuff. I could afford the real thing but then I would have to sacrifice a lot of other parts in my life. I do suffer from Leica envy inasmuch as I'd love to have some lenses. But I've no interest in a M9. I don't think that the rangefinder technology is my cup of tea.

I've presented all the lenses here before. They are Heliar 15mm f/4.5, 25mm f/2.8 ZM-Biogon and M-Rokkor 40mm f/2 The small black camera is a Ricoh GXR body with Mount A12 sensor. It takes the same lens family as Leica M. And it is modern digital mirrorless camera with an electronic view-finder. My F- lenses are eagerly awaiting a new adapter. Here's some reading for those who are interested:

Ashwin Rao describes how he is able to do manual focus fast enough to do sports photography. That impressed me, and that was one of the factors that settled the issue for me. Sean Reid was the one who convinced me that anti-aliasing filters are detrimental for image quality. Luminous Landscape's The field report convinced me about the user interface.
